The Story Behind Tribal Tales

How one encounter changed everything and inspired a mission to bridge cultures through authentic art.

1

The Encounter

2019, Rajasthan, India

It all began in a small village in Rajasthan where I met Lakshmi, a 65-year-old master weaver. She had been creating beautiful handwoven textiles for over 40 years, but struggled to sell them beyond her local market. Her intricate patterns told stories of her ancestors, but these stories were slowly fading away.

"My grandmother taught me these patterns when I was just a child. Each thread carries the memory of our people. But now, the young ones don't want to learn. They say there's no money in weaving."

The Realization

2020, During the Pandemic

As the world went into lockdown, I realized that artisans like Lakshmi were hit hardest. Their traditional markets closed, and they had no way to reach customers online. Meanwhile, people around the world were looking for authentic, meaningful art to bring beauty into their homes during difficult times.

85% of artisans lost income during lockdown
60% had no online presence

The Impact

2024, Today

Today, Tribal Tales has connected over 200 indigenous artisans with customers in 45 countries. Lakshmi's weavings now grace homes from New York to Tokyo, and she's training the next generation of weavers. Her story is just one of many.
